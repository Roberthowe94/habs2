HAB Identification Guide

What to Look For:

  • Blue-green, green, or reddish colored water
  • Scums, mats, or algae on the surface
  • Musty or earthy odors
  • Dead fish or other aquatic life

Safety Protocols:

  • Avoid contact with affected water
  • Don't let pets drink from or swim in affected areas
  • Report suspected blooms immediately
  • Seek medical attention if exposed

Understanding Water Quality

Key Water Quality Parameters

Temperature

Affects oxygen levels and aquatic life. Normal range: 15-25°C

pH Level

Measures acidity/alkalinity. Normal range: 6.5-8.5

Dissolved Oxygen

Essential for aquatic life. Normal range: >5 mg/L

Turbidity

Measures water clarity. Lower values indicate clearer water

Know It, Avoid It, Report It

KNOW IT

Learn to identify harmful algal blooms by their appearance and smell

AVOID IT

Stay away from affected water and keep pets away

REPORT IT

Report suspected blooms to help protect the community

Watershed Protection Tips

Reduce Nutrient Runoff

  • Use phosphorus-free detergents
  • Properly maintain septic systems
  • Create buffer zones near water

Sustainable Practices

  • Minimize lawn fertilizer use
  • Plant native vegetation
  • Properly dispose of pet waste
